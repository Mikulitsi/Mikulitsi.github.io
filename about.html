About Floorball

What is Floorball?

Floorball is a simple indoor sport where five players per team use sticks to hit a light plastic ball into a goal. It’s fast, fun, and perfect for newbies. Floorball is a fast-paced indoor team sport that blends speed, skill, and strategy. It originated in Sweden in the late 1960s and is now played in over 70 countries.

Background

The game features five field players and a goalkeeper per team on a 40 x 20 meter court, surrounded by a 50 cm high rink made of plastic mats. Players use sticks (half their body length) made of glass or carbon fiber, with blades curved up to 30 mm. The ball weighs 23 grams, has a 72 mm diameter with 26 holes, and is played into 115 x 160 cm goals. Two referees manage the game.

Floorball Equipment

Item Description
Stick Half your body length, blade curve up to 30 mm
Ball 23 grams, 72 mm diameter, 26 holes
Goal 115 x 160 cm
Floorball eyewear Mandatory in Finland for players born after 1998 when playing official matches. Not mandatory in other countries.

Rules

The match lasts 3 x 20-minute periods (or 3 x 15 for juniors), with time stopping during breaks. Key rules include:

  • No tackling, but shoulder-to-shoulder contact is allowed.
  • Sticks are for playing the ball only; misuse results in fouls.
  • Fouls lead to free hits or penalties (2, 2+2, or 10 minutes) or penalty shots.
  • Substitutions can happen on the fly or during stoppages, like ice hockey.
  • No offside rule applies.

How Floorball differs from Ice Hockey

Rule Floorball Ice Hockey
Offside No offside rule Has offside rule
Contact No tackling, shoulder contact allowed Allows more physical checking
Surface Indoor court of plastic mat Ice rink
Equipment Plastic ball & use of indoor shoes Puck & use of skates

How to Start Playing

  1. Find a local Floorball club (check the Contact page).
  2. Get a stick and a ball (they’re affordable and easy to find).
  3. Join a beginner session to learn the basics.
